Top Attractions in Tel Aviv

Discover the must-visit places that make Tel Aviv special.

Old Jaffa

Ancient port city with charming alleyways, artists' quarters, and historical significance.

Rothschild Boulevard

Iconic street lined with Bauhaus architecture, cafes, and cultural landmarks.

Carmel Market

Vibrant marketplace offering fresh produce, spices, clothing, and authentic local experiences.

Tel Aviv Museum of Art

Leading art museum featuring Israeli and international modern and contemporary art.

Food & Drink in Tel Aviv

Tel Aviv's culinary scene is diverse, innovative, and delicious.

Local Specialties

  • Hummus

    Creamy chickpea spread served with fresh pita bread.

  • Falafel

    Deep-fried chickpea balls, often served in pita with salad and tahini.

  • Shakshuka

    Eggs poached in a spiced tomato and pepper sauce.

  • Sabich

    Pita sandwich with fried eggplant, hard-boiled egg, tahini, and Israeli salad.

Getting Around Tel Aviv

Navigate the city with ease using these transportation options.

Public Transportation

Tel Aviv has an extensive bus network. Purchase a Rav-Kav card for all public transport options.

Cost: ₪5.5 per ride

Tel-O-Fun Bikes

Rent a bicycle from numerous stations throughout the city.

Cost: From ₪17 for daily access

Electric Scooters

Several companies offer electric scooter rentals via mobile apps.

Cost: ₪5 to unlock + ₪0.5 per minute

Taxis

Widely available throughout the city. Use the Gett app for convenience.

Cost: Starting from ₪35-40 for short trips
